Sodom & Gomorrah: Sin, Judgment & Rescue

REF Genesis 18 Genesis 19

The story begins when three heavenly visitors come to Abraham. One is understood by many Christians to be the Lord Himself — a pre-incarnate appearance of Christ — while the other two are angels who later go to Sodom.

1Three Visitors Come to Abraham

Genesis 18:1–8

One day Abraham was sitting at the entrance of his tent near the great trees of Mamre during the heat of the day. Suddenly he saw three men standing nearby.

Abraham quickly ran to greet them and bowed respectfully. He said:

"My Lord, if I have found favor in your sight, please do not pass by your servant."

He offered them:

  • Water to wash their feet
  • Rest under the tree
  • Food to eat

Abraham hurried and prepared a meal:

🍞
Sarah baked fresh bread
🐂
A young calf was prepared
🥛
Butter and milk were served

The visitors ate while Abraham stood nearby.

2The Promise of Isaac

Genesis 18:9–15

The visitors asked: "Where is your wife Sarah?" Abraham said she was in the tent. Then the Lord said:

"I will surely return to you about this time next year, and Sarah your wife will have a son."

Sarah was listening inside the tent. But Sarah and Abraham were very old. So Sarah laughed to herself and thought:

"After I am worn out and my lord is old, will I now have this pleasure?"

The Lord asked Abraham:

"Why did Sarah laugh? Is anything too hard for the Lord?"

Sarah became afraid and said she did not laugh. But the Lord said: "Yes, you did laugh."

3The Lord Reveals His Plan About Sodom

Genesis 18:16–21

The three visitors began walking toward Sodom. Abraham walked with them for some distance. Then the Lord said:

"Shall I hide from Abraham what I am about to do?"

God said that the cry against Sodom and Gomorrah was very great because their sin was extremely serious. So the Lord said He would go down and see how terrible the sin was.

At that point:

  • Two angels continued toward Sodom
  • The Lord remained with Abraham

This begins the famous conversation between Abraham and God.

4Abraham Pleads for Sodom

Genesis 18:22–33

Abraham understood that God was planning to destroy Sodom and Gomorrah. So Abraham boldly approached God and began interceding — especially for his nephew Lot, who lived in Sodom.

"Will you sweep away the righteous with the wicked?"

Abraham negotiated six times:

🙏 50 "If I find 50 righteous people, I will spare the whole city." ✓ Spared
🙏 45 "I will not destroy it if I find 45." ✓ Spared
🙏 40 "For the sake of 40, I will not destroy it." ✓ Spared
🙏 30 "I will not destroy it if I find 30." ✓ Spared
🙏 20 "For the sake of 20, I will not destroy it." ✓ Spared
🙏 10 "For the sake of 10, I will not destroy it." ✓ Spared

Then the Lord left, and Abraham returned home.

5The Two Angels Arrive in Sodom

Genesis 19:1–3

That evening the two angels reached Sodom. Lot was sitting at the city gate. When Lot saw them, he:

  • Stood up
  • Bowed down
  • Urged them strongly to stay in his house

At first the angels said they would stay in the city square. But Lot insisted strongly, so they went to his house. Lot prepared a meal and they ate.

6Wicked Men of Sodom

Genesis 19:4–11

Before they went to sleep, men from the city surrounded Lot's house. The Bible says: both young and old, all the men of the city. They shouted:

"Where are the men who came to you tonight? Bring them out to us!"

Their intention was evil. Lot went outside and closed the door behind him. Shockingly, Lot said:

"Please do not do this wicked thing."

He even offered his two daughters instead, but the crowd refused. The mob became violent and tried to break the door. Then the angels pulled Lot back inside and struck the men with blindness, so they could not find the door.

7The Angels Warn Lot

Genesis 19:12–14

The angels told Lot:

"Take your family and leave this place, because the Lord is about to destroy the city."

Lot went to warn his sons-in-law, who were engaged to his daughters. But they thought he was joking and did not believe him.

8The Escape From Sodom

Genesis 19:15–22

At dawn the angels urged Lot:

"Hurry! Take your wife and daughters and leave!"

But Lot hesitated. So the angels took them by the hand and led them out of the city. They said:

"Run for your lives! Do not look back!"

Lot asked permission to flee to a small town called Zoar, and the angels allowed it.

9Destruction of Sodom and Gomorrah

Genesis 19:23–25
🔥
The Lord rained fire and sulfur from heaven
After Lot reached safety in Zoar

The cities were completely destroyed. Everything was destroyed:

🏙️ The cities
👤 The people
🌿 The vegetation

10Lot's Wife Turns Into Salt

Genesis 19:26

While escaping, Lot's wife looked back toward Sodom. Because of this disobedience, she became a pillar of salt.

11Abraham Sees the Destruction

Genesis 19:27–29

Early the next morning Abraham went to the place where he had spoken with the Lord. He looked toward Sodom and Gomorrah. He saw dense smoke rising like smoke from a furnace.

But God remembered Abraham and saved Lot from the destruction.

12Lot and His Daughters in the Cave

Genesis 19:30–38

Lot left Zoar and lived in the mountains with his two daughters. They lived in a cave.

The daughters believed the whole world had been destroyed and there were no men left for them to marry. So they made a plan.

First Night
The older daughter said: "Let us get our father drunk and sleep with him so we can preserve our family line." They gave Lot wine. He became drunk. The older daughter slept with him, and Lot did not know it.
Second Night
The next day the older daughter told the younger one to do the same. They again gave Lot wine. The younger daughter slept with him. Again Lot did not realize it.

Birth of two nations:

Both daughters became pregnant and gave birth to sons:

Moab
Ancestor of the Moabites
Ben-Ammi
Ancestor of the Ammonites

These nations later became enemies of Israel.

Important Spiritual Lessons

1God Is Just
God does not destroy without reason. The sin of Sodom was extremely great.
2God Listens to Intercession
Abraham's conversation shows how God listens to prayer for others.
3God Rescues the Righteous
Because of Abraham, Lot was saved.
4Obedience Matters
Lot's wife looked back and lost her life.
5Sin Has Consequences
The actions of Lot's daughters led to the origins of nations that later opposed Israel.
