Gideon: Faith, Fear & Victory

REF Judges 6–8

1Israel Turns Away From God Again

Judges 6:1–6

After some years of peace, the people of Israel again began to do evil in the sight of God. They started worshipping idols and forgot the Lord.

Because of this, God allowed the Midianites to rule over Israel for seven years. The Midianites were very cruel to Israel.

What they did:

  • Every year when the Israelites planted crops, the Midianites came like a swarm of locusts
  • They brought huge numbers of people and animals
  • They destroyed crops and stole food
  • They left nothing for the Israelites to eat

Because of this oppression:

  • Israelites hid in mountain caves and strongholds
  • They were extremely poor and afraid

Finally, the Israelites cried out to God for help.

2God Sends a Prophet to Explain the Problem

Judges 6:7–10

Before delivering them, God sent a prophet to remind Israel why this was happening.

The prophet told them:

  • God had rescued them from Egypt
  • God had given them the land of Canaan
  • God had commanded them not to worship the gods of the Amorites
  • But Israel did not obey

So the prophet reminded them that their suffering was the result of their disobedience.

3The Angel of the Lord Appears to Gideon

Judges 6:11–24

One day, Gideon was secretly threshing wheat in a winepress. Normally wheat was threshed in an open place, but Gideon hid because he was afraid the Midianites would steal the grain.

Suddenly, the Angel of the Lord appeared to him and said:

"The Lord is with you, mighty warrior."

Gideon was confused and replied:

If the Lord is with us, why are all these troubles happening? Where are the miracles our fathers told us about? It seems the Lord has abandoned us.

Then the Lord told him:

"Go in the strength you have and save Israel from Midian. I am sending you."

Gideon was shocked — his clan was the weakest in the tribe of Manasseh, and he was the least important in his family. But God promised:

"I will be with you. You will defeat the Midianites as if they were one man."

Gideon Asks for a Sign

Gideon prepared an offering — a young goat, unleavened bread, and broth — and placed them on a rock. The angel touched the offering with his staff. Fire suddenly came out of the rock and burned the offering. Then the angel disappeared.

Gideon realized he had seen the Angel of the Lord and was afraid. But God told him:

"Peace. Do not be afraid. You will not die."

Gideon built an altar there and called it "The Lord Is Peace."

4Gideon Destroys the Altar of Baal

Judges 6:25–32

That night God gave Gideon his first mission:

  • Tear down his father's altar of Baal
  • Cut down the Asherah pole beside it
  • Build a proper altar to the Lord
  • Offer a bull as sacrifice using the wood from the Asherah pole

Gideon obeyed, but he was afraid of the townspeople, so he did it at night with ten servants.

The Town Gets Angry

In the morning, the people saw that the altar of Baal was destroyed and a new altar to the Lord was built. They demanded Gideon be killed. But his father Joash defended him:

"If Baal is really a god, let him defend himself!"

After that, Gideon received the nickname Jerubbaal, meaning "Let Baal fight against him."

5The Spirit of the Lord Comes on Gideon

Judges 6:33–35

Soon the enemy armies gathered again. The Midianites, Amalekites, and other eastern peoples crossed the Jordan River and camped in the Valley of Jezreel.

Then the Spirit of the Lord came upon Gideon. He blew a trumpet to call people to battle. Men from several tribes joined him:

  • Manasseh
  • Asher
  • Zebulun
  • Naphtali

An army began to gather.

6Gideon Asks for the Sign of the Fleece

Judges 6:36–40

Even though Gideon believed God, he still wanted reassurance. He asked God for a sign using a fleece of wool.

First Sign

If you will save Israel through me, make the fleece wet with dew but keep the ground dry.

In the morning, the fleece was so wet Gideon squeezed out a bowl of water — and the ground was dry.

Second Sign

Let the fleece be dry. Let the ground be covered with dew.

The next morning the fleece was dry and the ground was wet with dew. This confirmed to Gideon that God truly planned to deliver Israel through him.

7God Reduces Gideon's Army

Judges 7:1–8

Gideon gathered 32,000 soldiers. But God said:

"You have too many men. If Israel wins, they will think they saved themselves."

First Reduction

Anyone who was afraid could go home. 22,000 men left. Only 10,000 remained.

Second Reduction

God tested them at a river. The men drank water in two ways:

  • Some knelt down and drank normally
  • Others scooped water with their hands while staying alert

Only 300 men drank with their hands. God chose these 300 men. The remaining 9,700 men were sent home.

8Gideon Sneaks Into the Enemy Camp

Judges 7:9–15

That night God told Gideon:

"Go down to the enemy camp and listen."

Gideon went with his servant Purah. They heard a Midianite soldier telling a dream:

A loaf of barley bread rolled into the camp. It struck a tent and knocked it down.

Another soldier explained:

"This is the sword of Gideon. God has given Midian into his hand."

When Gideon heard this, he worshipped God. Then he returned to his army with confidence.

9The Battle of the 300 Men

Judges 7:16–25

Gideon divided the 300 men into three groups. Each man carried:

  • A trumpet
  • An empty jar
  • A torch inside the jar

At midnight they surrounded the enemy camp. Gideon gave the signal. The soldiers blew their trumpets, broke their jars, raised their torches, and shouted:

"A sword for the Lord and for Gideon!"

The Midianite camp woke up in panic. Because of confusion and darkness, the Midianites began fighting each other. Many ran away.

Israel chased them and captured two Midianite leaders:

  • Oreb
  • Zeeb

They were defeated.

10Gideon Confronts the Tribe of Ephraim

Judges 8:1–3

After the battle, the tribe of Ephraim became angry. They complained:

"Why didn't you call us earlier to fight?"

Gideon answered them wisely and peacefully. He praised them by saying their victories were greater than his. This calmed their anger.

11Gideon Pursues the Remaining Kings

Judges 8:4–21

Gideon and his 300 tired soldiers continued chasing the enemy kings Zebah and Zalmunna. On the way they asked two Israelite towns for food:

  • Succoth
  • Penuel

But both towns refused to help because they were afraid of Midian. Gideon warned them he would punish them later.

Eventually Gideon captured the two kings. Then he returned and punished the towns that refused help. Finally, Gideon executed the enemy kings.

12Israel Wants Gideon to Become King

Judges 8:22–23

After the victory, the Israelites said to Gideon:

"Rule over us — you, your son, and your grandson."

They wanted to make Gideon their king. But Gideon refused and said:

"I will not rule over you. The Lord will rule over you."

13Gideon Makes a Golden Ephod

Judges 8:24–27

Gideon asked the soldiers to give him gold earrings taken from the enemy. He used the gold to make an ephod (a religious garment or object). He placed it in his hometown Ophrah.

Unfortunately, the people began to treat the ephod like an idol. Israel again turned away from God. Even Gideon's family became involved in this mistake.

14The Last Years of Gideon

Judges 8:28–35

After Midian was defeated, the land had peace for 40 years. Gideon lived in his home. He had many wives, 70 sons, and a son named Abimelech through a concubine.

When Gideon died, Israel again turned to idol worship. They forgot the Lord. They also forgot the good Gideon had done for them.

Main Lessons From Gideon's Story

God can use weak people
Gideon thought he was the weakest, but God chose him.
Victory comes from God, not numbers
300 men defeated a huge army.
God patiently strengthens weak faith
Gideon asked for signs, and God helped him.
Success can still lead to new spiritual dangers
The ephod later became an idol.
People often forget God's help quickly
After Gideon died, Israel forgot the Lord again.
