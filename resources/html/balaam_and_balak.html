Balaam & Balak: Curse Turned to Blessing

REF Numbers 22–24 Numbers 25 Numbers 31

This story happens when the Israelites were traveling toward the Promised Land and camped near Moab. The Moabite king Balak became afraid and tried to use a prophet named Balaam to curse Israel. But God turned the curse into a blessing.

1Israel Camps Near Moab

Numbers 22:1

After many journeys in the wilderness, the Israelites camped in the plains of Moab, near the Jordan River, opposite Jericho.

King Balak, the king of Moab, saw how powerful Israel had become. He had also heard what Israel did to the Amorite kings Sihon and Og.

Because of this:

  • The Moabites were terrified
  • They believed Israel would destroy them

So Balak decided to stop Israel using spiritual power instead of military power.

2Balak Sends for Balaam

Numbers 22:2–7

Balak knew about a famous prophet named Balaam, who lived near the Euphrates River. People believed: whoever Balaam blessed would be blessed, and whoever he cursed would be cursed.

So Balak sent messengers with money to Balaam. They told him:

"A people has come out of Egypt and they cover the face of the land. Come and curse them for me, because they are too powerful for me."

Balak hoped:

  • Balaam would curse Israel
  • Then Moab could defeat them in battle

3Balaam Asks God

Numbers 22:8–13

Balaam asked the messengers to stay overnight. That night God spoke to Balaam and asked: "Who are these men with you?" Balaam explained Balak's request.

Then God clearly commanded:

  • Do not go with them
  • Do not curse Israel
  • Israel is blessed

So Balaam told Balak's messengers:

"The Lord has refused to let me go with you."

The messengers returned to Balak.

4Balak Tries Again With Greater Rewards

Numbers 22:15–21

Balak did not give up. He sent more important princes and promised Balaam great honor, great riches, and anything he asked. But Balaam replied:

"Even if Balak gave me his house full of silver and gold, I cannot go beyond the command of the Lord."

However, Balaam still asked God again. This time God said Balaam may go with them, but he must only speak what God tells him. So Balaam went with the Moabite officials.

5The Angel Blocks Balaam

Numbers 22:22–35

Although God allowed Balaam to go, God was angry because Balaam's heart desired the reward. So an angel of the Lord stood in the road with a drawn sword. But Balaam could not see the angel — only his donkey saw it.

1
First time: The donkey turned off the road into a field. Balaam became angry and beat the donkey.
2
Second time: The angel stood in a narrow path between vineyards. The donkey pressed against a wall, crushing Balaam's foot. Again Balaam beat the donkey.
3
Third time: The angel stood where there was no place to turn. The donkey lay down under Balaam. Balaam became furious and beat the donkey with a stick.

6The Donkey Speaks

Numbers 22:28–30

Then something amazing happened. God opened the donkey's mouth, and it spoke!

🐴
Donkey
"What have I done to you that you have beaten me these three times?"
😠
Balaam
"You made a fool of me! If I had a sword, I would kill you!"
🐴
Donkey
"Have I ever behaved this way before?"
😶
Balaam
"No."

7Balaam Finally Sees the Angel

Numbers 22:31–35

Then God opened Balaam's eyes. He saw the angel with a drawn sword standing in the road. The angel said:

"Your path is reckless before me."

The angel explained that the donkey had saved Balaam's life. If the donkey had not turned aside, the angel would have killed Balaam.

Balaam repented and offered to return home. But the angel said:

"Go with the men, but speak only what I tell you."

8Balaam Meets Balak

Numbers 22:36–41

Balak came to meet Balaam and said: "Why did you not come earlier? Am I not able to honor you?"

Balaam replied:

"I cannot say anything except what God tells me."

Balak then took Balaam to high places where he could see part of Israel's camp.

9First Attempt to Curse Israel

Numbers 23:1–12
1 First Attempt — Mount Bamoth Baal
Balaam asked Balak to build 7 altars and sacrifice 7 bulls and 7 rams. After the sacrifice, Balaam sought God's message. Instead of a curse, God gave a blessing. Balaam said:

"How can I curse those whom God has not cursed?"

He declared that Israel is a people set apart and their future is blessed. Balak was frustrated.

10Second Attempt to Curse Israel

Numbers 23:13–26
2 Second Attempt — Field of Zophim
Balak thought maybe the location was the problem. He took Balaam to another mountain. Again they built 7 altars and sacrifices. Again Balaam asked God. But God again gave a blessing.

"God is not a man that He should lie."

He declared: Israel is blessed, God will not change His mind, and no curse can work against Israel. Balak became angry.

11Third Attempt

Numbers 23:27 – 24:9
3 Third Attempt — Top of Peor
Balak tried one more place. But this time Balaam did not even try to curse. The Spirit of God came upon him, and he prophesied blessings.

He described Israel as:

  • Beautiful camps
  • A prosperous nation
  • Strong like a lion

"Blessed are those who bless you, and cursed are those who curse you."

12Balak Becomes Furious

Numbers 24:10–14

Balak became extremely angry. He clapped his hands and said:

"I called you to curse my enemies, but you have blessed them three times!"

He told Balaam to go home without reward. Balaam replied:

"Did I not tell you I can only speak what the Lord says?"

13Balaam's Final Prophecies

Numbers 24:15–25

Before leaving, Balaam gave more prophecies about the future. One famous prophecy says:

"A star will come out of Jacob; a scepter will rise out of Israel."

Many believe this prophecy points to the coming Messiah. He also predicted the defeat of several nations:

  • Moab
  • Edom
  • Amalek
  • Others

After this, Balaam returned home.

⚠️
What Balaam Did Later — That Caused Israel to Sin
Numbers 25 & Numbers 31

Even though Balaam could not curse Israel directly, he found another way to cause harm.

What happened in Numbers 25:

The Israelite men began to sin with Moabite women. They also started worshipping the Moabite god Baal of Peor. This brought God's anger against Israel.

Balaam's role — revealed in Numbers 31:

Later, when Israel defeated Midian, the Bible reveals the truth. Balaam had given advice to the Moabites and Midianites to use women to seduce the Israelite men into sexual sin and idol worship. This was Balaam's strategy — since he could not curse Israel from outside, he caused them to sin from within.

"These women, on Balaam's advice, caused the Israelites to act unfaithfully against the Lord in the matter of Peor."

Because of this, Balaam was eventually killed in battle against Midian (Numbers 31:8).

Why Balaam Is Called a False Prophet in the New Testament

2 Peter 2:15 · Jude 1:11 · Revelation 2:14

In the New Testament, Balaam's name is used as a warning. Three different writers mention him as an example of a false prophet.

📖 2 Peter 2:15
Peter warns about false teachers who follow the "way of Balaam" — meaning people who use religion for personal profit and greed. Balaam loved money so much that he kept looking for ways to please Balak despite God's clear command.
📖 Jude 1:11
Jude warns that false teachers have rushed into the "error of Balaam" — meaning they compromise God's truth for reward and selfish gain.
📖 Revelation 2:14
Jesus rebukes the church at Pergamum for holding on to the "teaching of Balaam" — allowing sexual immorality and idol worship inside the church, just as Balaam taught the Moabites to lead Israel into sin.

Why is he called a false prophet?

  • He knew God's voice but still desired money and honor
  • He tried multiple times to find a way to curse Israel
  • He finally gave evil advice to destroy Israel from within through sin
  • He used spiritual gifts for personal gain, not for God's glory

The "way of Balaam" became a phrase in the New Testament to describe anyone who mixes truth with greed, compromise, and spiritual corruption.

Important Lessons From the Story

1God Protects His People
Even when enemies try to curse Israel, God turns the curse into blessing.
2God Controls Prophecy
Even a prophet hired to curse cannot go against God's will.
3Greed Is Dangerous
Balaam wanted money and honor, and this caused him trouble and eventually his death.
4God Can Use Anything
God even used a donkey to speak and correct Balaam.
5God's Blessing Cannot Be Reversed
What God blesses, no human can curse.
