The Sermon on the Mount

REF Matthew 5–7

Introduction

Matthew 5–7

The Sermon on the Mount is one of the most important teachings of Jesus in the Bible. It is found in Matthew chapters 5, 6, and 7.

Jesus went up on a mountain and many people came to listen. His disciples sat near Him, and Jesus began to teach them how a person should live if they belong to the Kingdom of God.

These teachings explain:

  • How God wants us to live
  • What true righteousness is
  • How our heart should be, not only our actions

1The Beatitudes

Matthew 5:1–12

Jesus started by describing the kind of people who are blessed by God. "Blessed" means happy in God's eyes.

1. Blessed are the poor in spirit
Meaning: People who know they need God.
Example: Someone who humbly prays, "Lord, I cannot live without You."
Reward: The Kingdom of Heaven is theirs.
2. Blessed are those who mourn
Meaning: People who feel sorrow for sin and suffering.
Example: Someone who cries because the world is full of evil.
Reward: God will comfort them.
3. Blessed are the meek
Meek means gentle, humble, not proud.
Example: A person who does not fight for power but trusts God.
Reward: They will inherit the earth.
4. Blessed are those who hunger and thirst for righteousness
Meaning: People who strongly desire to do what is right.
Example: Someone who wants to obey God more than anything.
Reward: God will satisfy them.
5. Blessed are the merciful
Meaning: People who forgive and show kindness.
Example: Forgiving someone who hurt you.
Reward: God will show mercy to them.
6. Blessed are the pure in heart
Meaning: People whose heart is clean and sincere.
Example: Serving God without pretending.
Reward: They will see God.
7. Blessed are the peacemakers
Meaning: People who bring peace between others.
Example: Helping two people stop fighting.
Reward: They will be called children of God.
8. Blessed are those persecuted for righteousness
Meaning: People who suffer because they follow God.
Example: Someone mocked for living a godly life.
Reward: The Kingdom of Heaven is theirs.

2Salt and Light

Matthew 5:13–16

Jesus said believers are like salt and light.

Salt

  • Salt preserves food and gives taste.
  • Meaning: Christians must influence the world with goodness.
  • If salt loses its taste, it becomes useless — if believers stop living for God, they lose their purpose.

Light

  • Light removes darkness.
  • Meaning: Believers must show God through their life — doing good, helping others, living honestly.

Jesus said:

"Let your light shine before others." So people will see your good works and glorify God.

3Jesus and the Law

Matthew 5:17–20

Jesus said:

"I did not come to destroy the law but to fulfill it."

Meaning: Jesus completed the true meaning of God's law. The Pharisees followed rules outwardly, but Jesus taught that God looks at the heart.

4Anger = Beginning of Murder

Matthew 5:21–26

The law said: "Do not murder." But Jesus explained deeper.

If someone hates, insults, or keeps anger — it begins the same sin in the heart.

So Jesus taught: First make peace with others, then worship God.

5Lust = Adultery in the Heart

Matthew 5:27–30

The law said: "Do not commit adultery."

Jesus said even looking with sinful desire is adultery in the heart.

Meaning: Sin starts in the mind and heart before actions. Jesus taught us to remove anything that causes sin.

6Divorce Teaching

Matthew 5:31–32

In that time many men divorced wives easily. Jesus said marriage is a serious covenant. Divorce should not happen for selfish reasons.

7Do Not Take False Oaths

Matthew 5:33–37

People used to swear promises like:

  • "I swear by heaven"
  • "I swear by the temple"

Jesus said:

Just speak truth. Let your Yes mean Yes, and No mean No.

8Do Not Take Revenge

Matthew 5:38–42

Old law said: "Eye for an eye" — punishment equal to harm.

Jesus taught something greater: Do not seek revenge.

  • If someone slaps you → do not fight back
  • If someone forces you to go one mile → go two miles
  • Give to those who ask

Meaning: Respond with grace instead of revenge.

9Love Your Enemies

Matthew 5:43–48

People believed: love friends, hate enemies. But Jesus said:

Love your enemies and pray for them.

Why? Because God shows kindness to both good and evil people. If we only love people who love us, that is easy. But loving enemies shows true godly love.

10Giving to the Needy

Matthew 6:1–4

Jesus warned against showing off religion. Some people helped the poor just to get praise.

Jesus said:

"Do not let your left hand know what your right hand is doing." Help others secretly — God sees and will reward.

11Prayer

Matthew 6:5–15

Some people prayed loudly in public to impress others. Jesus said: pray privately and sincerely.

The Lord's Prayer

Our Father in heaven

Your name be holy

Your kingdom come

Your will be done on earth as in heaven

Give us today our daily bread

Forgive our sins as we forgive others

Do not lead us into temptation

Deliver us from evil

Prayer should include worship, trust, forgiveness, and dependence on God.

12Fasting

Matthew 6:16–18

Some people fasted and looked sad so others would notice. Jesus said: fast quietly, do not show off. God sees the heart.

13Treasures in Heaven

Matthew 6:19–24

Jesus said: do not store treasures only on earth. Earthly things can be destroyed:

🦋
Moth eats and ruins your clothes
🔩
Rust corrodes and destroys your valuables
🦹
Thieves break in and steal what you own

But treasure in heaven lasts forever. How to store heavenly treasure?

  • Serving God
  • Loving others
  • Doing righteousness

14Do Not Worry

Matthew 6:25–34

Jesus taught about worry.

  • Birds – God feeds them
  • Flowers – God clothes them beautifully

If God cares for them, He will care for you more.

Jesus said:

Seek first the Kingdom of God. Then God will provide what you need.

15Do Not Judge Others

Matthew 7:1–6

Jesus warned about judging others.

You see a small speck in someone's eye — but you have a big log in your own eye.

Meaning: First correct your own life. Then help others.

16Ask, Seek, Knock

Matthew 7:7–11

Jesus encouraged prayer.

  • Ask → it will be given
  • Seek → you will find
  • Knock → the door will open

God is a good Father who gives good things to His children.

17The Golden Rule

Matthew 7:12

One of the most famous teachings.

"Do to others what you want them to do to you."

  • If you want kindness → be kind
  • If you want respect → give respect

This summarizes much of God's law.

18The Narrow and Wide Gate

Matthew 7:13–14

Jesus described two roads.

Wide road

  • Easy — many people go there
  • Leads to destruction

Narrow road

  • Difficult — few find it
  • Leads to life

Following Jesus requires discipline and commitment.

19False Prophets

Matthew 7:15–20

Jesus warned about false teachers. They may look good outside — but you recognize them by their fruit (their actions and character).

  • Good tree → good fruit
  • Bad tree → bad fruit

20True and False Disciples

Matthew 7:21–23

Some people say: "Lord, Lord." But they do not obey God.

Jesus said obedience matters, not just words.

21The Wise and Foolish Builders

Matthew 7:24–27

Jesus ended the sermon with a story.

Wise man

  • Builds his house on rock
  • When storms come, the house stands
  • Meaning: People who hear Jesus and obey Him

Foolish man

  • Builds his house on sand
  • When storms come, the house collapses
  • Meaning: People who hear but do not obey
