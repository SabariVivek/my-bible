Samson: Strength, Weakness & Redemption

REF Judges 13–16

This story tells how God raised a man with extraordinary strength to deliver Israel from the Philistines, but also shows how Samson's personal weaknesses led to his downfall.

1Israel Oppressed by the Philistines

Judges 13:1

The story begins during a time when the Israelites again turned away from God. Because of their disobedience, God allowed the Philistines to rule over Israel. This oppression lasted 40 years. During this time, God planned to raise a deliverer.

2The Angel Announces Samson's Birth

Judges 13:2–7

In the town of Zorah, there lived a man named Manoah and his wife.

Important detail:

Manoah's wife could not have children.

One day the Angel of the Lord appeared to her and told her she would give birth to a special son. The angel gave strict instructions:

Nazirite Vow — from birth
  • She must not drink wine or strong drink
  • She must not eat anything unclean
  • The child would be a Nazirite dedicated to God from birth
  • No razor must touch his head
  • He would begin to deliver Israel from the Philistines

A Nazirite vow meant a life dedicated to God.

3Manoah Asks for Guidance

Judges 13:8–14

When Manoah heard about this visitor, he prayed:

"Lord, please let the man of God come again and teach us how to raise the child."

God answered the prayer. The angel appeared again to Manoah's wife. She quickly ran and brought Manoah. Manoah asked: "How should we raise this child?" The angel repeated the instructions — the boy must be holy to God from birth.

4The Angel Reveals God's Power

Judges 13:15–23

Manoah offered to prepare food for the angel. But the angel said:

"If you offer something, offer it to the Lord."

So Manoah prepared a burnt offering. As the flame rose from the altar, the Angel of the Lord went up in the flame. Manoah and his wife fell to the ground in fear.

Manoah said: "We will surely die, because we have seen God!" But his wife wisely replied — if God wanted to kill them, He would not have accepted the offering or given them this promise.

5Samson Is Born

Judges 13:24–25

Soon the woman gave birth to a son. They named him Samson. The Bible says the child grew, the Lord blessed him, and the Spirit of the Lord began to move him. This happened between the towns of Zorah and Eshtaol.

6Samson Wants a Philistine Wife

Judges 14:1–4

When Samson grew older, he traveled to Timnah. There he saw a Philistine woman and wanted to marry her. He told his parents: "Get her for me as my wife."

His parents objected and asked: "Why marry a Philistine woman? Aren't there women among our people?" But they did not know that God was using this situation to begin conflict with the Philistines.

7Samson Kills a Lion

Judges 14:5–6

While traveling to Timnah with his parents, a young lion suddenly attacked Samson. Then the Spirit of the Lord came powerfully upon him. Samson tore the lion apart with his bare hands. He told no one about this.

8Honey in the Lion's Carcass

Judges 14:8–9

Later, Samson returned to marry the woman. He passed by the place where he killed the lion. Inside the lion's dead body, bees had made a hive and there was honey. Samson scooped out the honey, ate it while walking, and gave some to his parents — but he did not tell them it came from the lion's body. This was technically against the Nazirite purity rules.

9Samson's Wedding Feast and the Riddle

Judges 14:10–14

Samson held a wedding feast. At the feast, 30 Philistine men were chosen to accompany him. Samson proposed a riddle:

"Out of the eater came something to eat. Out of the strong came something sweet."

He promised: if they solved it in 7 days, he would give them 30 garments. If they failed, they must give him 30 garments. The riddle referred to the lion and the honey.

10The Philistines Pressure Samson's Wife

Judges 14:15–18

The Philistine men could not solve the riddle. So they threatened Samson's wife:

"If you do not find out the answer, we will burn you and your family."

She cried to Samson repeatedly until he finally told her. She then told the Philistine men. On the seventh day they answered:

"What is sweeter than honey? What is stronger than a lion?"

Samson realised they cheated and said:

"If you had not plowed with my heifer, you would not have solved my riddle."

11Samson Kills 30 Philistines

Judges 14:19

The Spirit of the Lord came upon Samson. He went to Ashkelon. There he killed 30 Philistine men, took their clothing, and gave them to the men who solved the riddle. Then he returned home in anger. His wife was given to another man.

12Samson Burns Philistine Fields

Judges 15:1–5

Later Samson returned to visit his wife. But her father said: "I gave her to your companion." Instead he offered Samson her younger sister. Samson became furious.

He captured 300 foxes, tied their tails together, and placed torches between them. He released them into the Philistine fields. The foxes burned:

  • Grain
  • Vineyards
  • Olive groves

13Philistines Burn Samson's Wife

Judges 15:6

The Philistines asked: "Who did this?" When they learned it was Samson, they burned his wife and her father to death. This fulfilled the threat they made earlier.

14Samson Slaughters Many Philistines

Judges 15:7–8

Samson said: "Since you acted like this, I will take revenge." He attacked them violently and killed many of them. Then he went to live in a cave at the Rock of Etam.

15Samson Kills 1,000 Men with a Donkey's Jawbone

Judges 15:9–17

The Philistines came to capture Samson and camped in Lehi. 3,000 men from Judah went to Samson and said: "Don't you know the Philistines rule over us?" They tied him up to deliver him.

But when Samson arrived, the Spirit of the Lord came upon him again. The ropes broke like burnt threads. He found the jawbone of a donkey. With it he killed 1,000 Philistine men. Samson then declared:

"With a donkey's jawbone I have made donkeys of them."

16God Provides Water

Judges 15:18–20

After the battle Samson became extremely thirsty. He cried out to God:

"Will I now die of thirst?"

God miraculously opened a spring. Samson drank and regained strength. He judged Israel 20 years during the Philistine oppression.

17Samson in Gaza

Judges 16:1–3

Samson later visited Gaza. The Philistines surrounded the city gates to capture him. But Samson woke up at midnight. He pulled up the city gates and carried them to the top of a hill near Hebron. This demonstrated his incredible strength.

18Samson and Delilah

Judges 16:4–5

Later Samson fell in love with a woman named Delilah. The Philistine rulers offered her 1,100 pieces of silver each if she discovered the secret of Samson's strength. She agreed.

19Delilah Tries to Discover the Secret

Judges 16:6–14

Delilah repeatedly asked Samson: "Tell me the secret of your strength." Samson gave false answers three times. Each time she tested him by calling: "Samson! The Philistines are upon you!" But he easily broke free.

1
Tie me with seven fresh bowstrings
2
Tie me with new ropes
3
Weave my hair into a loom

20Samson Reveals His Secret

Judges 16:15–17

Delilah kept pressuring him daily. Finally Samson told the truth:

  • He had been a Nazirite to God from birth
  • His hair had never been cut
  • If shaved, his strength would leave him

21Samson Is Captured

Judges 16:18–21

Delilah cut his hair while he slept. When the Philistines attacked, Samson tried to escape. But the Bible says:

"He did not know that the Lord had left him."

The Philistines captured him. They gouged out his eyes, put him in chains, and forced him to grind grain in prison.

22Samson's Hair Begins to Grow

Judges 16:22

But the Bible quietly notes something important: his hair began to grow again.

23The Philistines Celebrate

Judges 16:23–25

The Philistines held a great celebration to their god Dagon. They believed their god had defeated Samson. They brought Samson into the temple to entertain them. Thousands were gathered on the roof.

24Samson's Final Prayer

Judges 16:26–28

Samson asked the servant boy: "Let me lean against the pillars holding the temple." Then Samson prayed:

"Sovereign Lord, remember me. Strengthen me just once more."

25Samson Destroys the Temple

Judges 16:29–30

Samson placed his hands on the two central pillars. He pushed with all his strength. The temple collapsed. Thousands of Philistines died. The Bible says:

Samson killed more people in his death than in his life.

26Samson's Burial

Judges 16:31

His family came and carried his body back. They buried him between Zorah and Eshtaol in his father's tomb. Samson had judged Israel 20 years.

Main Lessons from Samson's Story

God can use imperfect people
Samson had great strength but lacked self-control. God still worked through him.
Disobedience weakens a person spiritually
Each compromise with the Nazirite vow brought Samson closer to defeat.
God still listens when we sincerely pray
Even after all his failures, God answered Samson's final prayer and restored his strength.
