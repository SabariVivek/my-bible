David and Goliath

REF 1 Samuel 17

1The War Between Israel and the Philistines

At that time, the nation of Israel was ruled by King Saul. The Israelites were often at war with their enemies, especially the Philistines.

One day the armies of Israel and the Philistines gathered for battle.

  • The Philistines camped on one hill
  • The Israelites camped on another hill
  • Between them was a valley called the Valley of Elah

Both armies stood ready for battle, but no one started fighting.

From the Philistine army came a champion warrior named Goliath. Goliath was enormous in size. The Bible describes him in detail:

Goliath stepped forward and shouted to the army of Israel:

"Why do you come out to fight? Choose one man to come down and fight me!"

He challenged them:

  • If the Israelite soldier defeated him, the Philistines would serve Israel
  • But if Goliath defeated the man, Israel would serve the Philistines

He then shouted proudly:

"I defy the armies of Israel today! Give me a man to fight!"

When King Saul and the Israelites heard this, they became very afraid.

3Goliath's Daily Challenge

For 40 days, morning and evening, Goliath came out and repeated the same challenge. Every time the Israelites heard him shouting, they ran away in fear. No one was brave enough to fight him.

4David Comes to the Battlefield

At that time, there was a young shepherd boy named David, the son of Jesse from Bethlehem. David had seven brothers, and his three oldest brothers were soldiers in Saul's army:

  • Eliab
  • Abinadab
  • Shammah

David was the youngest son. He usually stayed at home taking care of his father's sheep.

One day Jesse told David:

"Take this roasted grain and these ten loaves of bread to your brothers. Also take these ten cheeses to the commander of their unit. See how your brothers are doing and bring back news."

So early in the morning David left the sheep with another shepherd and went to the battlefield.

5David Hears Goliath

When David arrived, the armies were standing in battle formation shouting war cries. Just then Goliath came out again and shouted his usual challenge. David heard the giant mocking Israel and insulting the living God.

The soldiers told David: "This giant comes every day. Whoever kills him will receive great rewards from the king."

The rewards included:

💰 Great riches
👑 The king's daughter in marriage
📜 His family being free from taxes in Israel

David asked the soldiers again about the reward. His oldest brother Eliab became angry with him and said:

"Why have you come here? Who did you leave those few sheep with in the wilderness? I know your pride — you came only to watch the battle!"

But David replied calmly:

"What have I done now? I was only asking a question."

6David Speaks to King Saul

Eventually David's words were reported to King Saul, and Saul called for him. David said to Saul:

"Do not let anyone lose heart because of this Philistine. Your servant will go and fight him."

Saul looked at David and said:

"You cannot fight him. You are only a boy, and he has been a warrior since his youth."

But David answered:

"When I was taking care of my father's sheep, a lion or a bear sometimes came and carried off a sheep."

David continued:

"I chased it, struck it, and rescued the sheep. If the animal attacked me, I grabbed its hair, struck it, and killed it."

Then David said something powerful:

"The Lord who delivered me from the paw of the lion and the bear will deliver me from this Philistine."

Saul finally said:

"Go, and the Lord be with you."

7David Refuses Saul's Armor

Saul gave David his own armor:

  • A bronze helmet
  • A coat of armor
  • A sword

David tried to walk in them, but he was not used to them. So David said:

"I cannot fight with these. I am not used to them."

He removed the armor.

8David's Simple Weapons

Instead, David took:

  • His shepherd's staff
  • His sling
  • Five smooth stones from a stream

He put the stones in his shepherd's bag and walked toward the giant.

🪨
Why Did David Pick Five Stones?
1 Samuel 17:40 — A closer look

The Bible says David picked five smooth stones from the stream (1 Samuel 17:40). The Bible does not directly explain why the number was five, but there are a few simple and commonly understood reasons.

1In Case the First Stone Missed
The simplest reason is practical preparation. David knew he was going into battle, so he took extra stones as backup.
  • If the first stone missed, he could try again
  • A sling could shoot stones very quickly
  • Having several stones was normal for a sling fighter
So David was prepared, not careless.
2Goliath Had Brothers
Later in the Bible we learn that Goliath had four other giant relatives.
  • 📖 Second Book of Samuel 21:15–22
  • 📖 First Book of Chronicles 20:4–8
Some Bible teachers think David may have taken five stones because there were five giants in that family (Goliath + four relatives). So if others attacked, he was ready. However, the Bible does not clearly say this was the reason.
3David Was Prepared but Trusted God
David trusted God completely, but he still prepared for the battle. He did not say: "God will do everything, so I will bring nothing." Instead he took his sling, took five stones, and went forward trusting God. This shows a good balance:
Faith in God
Trusted the Lord completely for victory
Wise Preparation
Brought his sling and five stones ready

David picked five stones because:

1
He wanted extra stones in case he missed
2
Some think it relates to Goliath's giant relatives
3
It shows David prepared well but trusted God for victory

9Goliath Mocks David

When Goliath saw David coming toward him, he was insulted. David looked very young and had no armor. Goliath said:

"Am I a dog that you come at me with sticks?"

He cursed David by his gods and shouted:

"Come here, and I will give your flesh to the birds and the wild animals!"

10David's Powerful Reply

David answered Goliath with faith and courage:

"You come against me with sword, spear, and javelin, but I come against you in the name of the Lord Almighty, the God of the armies of Israel, whom you have defied."

David continued:

"Today the Lord will deliver you into my hands. I will strike you down and cut off your head. Then the whole world will know that there is a God in Israel."

He also said:

"The battle belongs to the Lord."

11The Battle

As Goliath moved closer to attack, David ran quickly toward the battle line. He reached into his bag, took out a stone, placed it in his sling, and swung it.

The stone flew through the air and struck Goliath on the forehead. The stone sank into his forehead, and the giant fell face down on the ground.

David defeated Goliath without a sword. Then David ran to Goliath, took the giant's own sword, and cut off his head.

12The Philistines Run Away

When the Philistine army saw their champion was dead, they became afraid and ran away. The Israelite army shouted loudly and chased the Philistines. They defeated them and plundered their camp.

David took Goliath's head and later placed the giant's weapons in his own tent.

13Saul Asks About David

After the battle, King Saul asked: "Whose son is this young man?" David was brought before Saul, still holding Goliath's head. David answered:

"I am the son of your servant Jesse of Bethlehem."

This victory made David famous throughout Israel.

Important Lesson from the Story

God does not look at size, strength, or weapons
God gives victory to those who trust Him. David's faith was stronger than Goliath's strength.
