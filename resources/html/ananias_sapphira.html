Ananias & Sapphira: Deception Before God

REF Acts 5:1–11

1What Was Happening in the Early Church

After Jesus rose from the dead and returned to heaven, the apostles began preaching the gospel. The believers in Jerusalem lived with great unity.

Important things about the early church:

  • They loved and helped each other
  • Some believers sold their land or houses
  • They brought the money to the apostles
  • The apostles distributed the money to people who were poor or in need

One famous example was Barnabas, who sold a field and gave the full money to the apostles. Because of this generosity, everyone respected those who gave honestly.

2Ananias and Sapphira's Plan

There was a married couple — Ananias and Sapphira. They also owned a piece of land. They decided to sell the land, but they made a secret plan.

Their plan:

  • Sell the land
  • Keep some of the money for themselves
  • Bring only part of the money to the apostles
  • Pretend that it was the full amount

Keeping money was not the sin. Their sin was lying to God and pretending to be more generous than they were. Both husband and wife agreed to do this.

3Ananias Brings the Money

Ananias came to the apostles and placed the money before them. The apostle Peter spoke to him very seriously:

Satan had filled his heart. He had lied to the apostles — but more importantly, he had lied to the Holy Spirit.

Peter explained:

The land belonged to you. Even after selling it, the money was still yours. Why did you decide to lie?

Peter made it clear — Ananias was not forced to give the money. The problem was deception.

4The Judgment on Ananias

When Ananias heard Peter's words:

  • He fell down immediately
  • He died on the spot

Young men came and wrapped his body, carried him out, and buried him.

Everyone who heard about this event became very afraid. They understood that God was protecting the purity of the church.

5Sapphira Comes Later

About three hours later, Sapphira came. She did not know what had happened to her husband. Peter asked her:

"Did you sell the land for this amount of money?"

She had a chance to tell the truth. But she repeated the lie:

"Yes, that is the full amount."

She chose to continue the deception.

6Peter Confronts Sapphira

Peter then said:

Why did you agree with your husband to test the Spirit of the Lord? The men who buried your husband are at the door. They will carry you out also.

Immediately Sapphira fell down and died. The young men came again and buried her beside her husband.

7The Result in the Church

After this event:

  • Great fear came upon the whole church
  • People realized that God sees everything
  • The church became more careful about honesty and holiness

The apostles continued preaching, and many miracles happened.

Important Lessons From the Story

1. God cares about honesty
Lying to God is very serious. God knows what is in our hearts.
2. Hypocrisy is dangerous
Ananias and Sapphira wanted the reputation of generosity without actually being generous. They wanted praise from people, not approval from God.
3. Giving to God must be sincere
God does not force people to give. But whatever we give must be truthful and sincere.
4. God protected the early church
The early church was just beginning. God removed sin quickly to keep the church pure and holy.

Simple Summary

Ananias and Sapphira sold land but secretly kept some money.
They lied and pretended to give the full amount to the apostles.
Peter, guided by the Holy Spirit, exposed their lie.
Both of them died as God's judgment.
This caused great fear and taught the church to live in honesty before God.
