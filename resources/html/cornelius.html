Cornelius: Salvation Comes to the Gentiles

REF Acts 10 Acts 11

This story is very important because it shows how God opened the way of salvation to the Gentiles (non-Jews). The events are told step by step in simple English.

1Who Was Cornelius?

Cornelius lived in Caesarea. He was a Roman centurion — a Roman army officer in charge of about 100 soldiers. Even though he was not Jewish (a Gentile, meaning non-Jew), he was a good and God-fearing man.

🙏
PrayerHe prayed to God regularly
💛
GenerosityHe gave money to help poor people
🏠
HouseholdHis whole family also respected God
✝️
FaithHe believed in God but had not yet heard the full gospel about Jesus

2Cornelius Receives a Vision

Acts 10:1–8

One day around 3 PM, Cornelius was praying. Suddenly an angel of God appeared and called his name:

"Cornelius!"

Cornelius became afraid and asked: "What is it, Lord?" The angel told him:

Your prayers have been heard by God. Your help to the poor has been remembered by God.

Instructions from the angel:

  • Send men to the city of Joppa
  • Find a man named Simon Peter
  • He is staying with Simon the tanner, whose house is near the sea
  • Bring Peter to your house

Cornelius immediately obeyed. He sent two servants and one soldier who respected God to travel to Joppa.

3Peter Receives a Vision from God

Acts 10:9–16

While Cornelius' men were traveling, something important happened to Peter. Around noon he went to the roof of the house to pray. While praying he became very hungry — then he fell into a vision.

What Peter saw:

The sky opened and something like a large sheet came down from heaven, tied at the four corners. Inside were many animals:

🦁 Wild animals
🦎 Reptiles
🐦 Birds

Many of these were considered unclean according to Jewish law. Then a voice said:

"Peter, get up. Kill and eat."

Peter refused: "No, Lord! I have never eaten anything unclean." But the voice replied:

"What God has made clean, do not call unclean."

This happened three times. Then the sheet went back into heaven. Peter was confused and wondered what the vision meant.

4Cornelius' Men Arrive

Acts 10:17–23

At that moment, the men sent by Cornelius arrived at the house. The Holy Spirit spoke to Peter:

Three men are looking for you. Go with them. Do not doubt.

Peter went down and met the men. They explained everything — Cornelius is a righteous man, an angel told him to bring you to his house, he wants to hear your message.

Peter invited them to stay the night. The next day he traveled with them to Caesarea.

5Peter Meets Cornelius

Acts 10:24–29

When Peter arrived at Cornelius' house, Cornelius fell at Peter's feet and tried to worship him. But Peter stopped him:

"Stand up! I am also a man."

Peter then told everyone something important. Normally, Jews did not associate with Gentiles. But Peter said:

"God has shown me that I should not call any person unclean."

This was the meaning of the vision of the animals. God was teaching Peter that the gospel is for all people.

6Cornelius Explains His Vision

Acts 10:30–33

Cornelius explained what happened four days earlier — he was praying, an angel appeared, and told him to send for Peter. Then he said:

"We are all here in the presence of God to listen to what the Lord has commanded you to say."

7Peter Preaches the Gospel

Acts 10:34–43

Peter began preaching about Jesus Christ:

God does not show favoritism. Anyone who fears God and does what is right is accepted by Him.

The gospel message Peter shared:

  • Jesus preached peace and performed miracles
  • Jesus healed many people
  • Jesus was crucified on a cross
  • God raised Him from the dead on the third day
  • Jesus appeared to witnesses after His resurrection
  • Everyone who believes in Jesus receives forgiveness of sins

8The Holy Spirit Comes on the Gentiles

Acts 10:44–46

While Peter was still speaking, something amazing happened — the Holy Spirit came upon everyone listening.

The Jewish believers who came with Peter were shocked, because the Gentiles also received the Holy Spirit. They began:

  • Speaking in other languages
  • Praising God

This showed clearly that God accepted the Gentiles.

9Cornelius and His Household Are Baptized

Acts 10:47–48

After seeing this, Peter said:

"Can anyone stop these people from being baptized?"

Since they received the Holy Spirit just like the Jewish believers, Peter commanded that they be baptized in the name of Jesus Christ. Cornelius and his whole household became believers. They asked Peter to stay with them for several days.

10Peter Explains the Event to the Church

Acts 11:1–18

When Peter returned to Jerusalem, some Jewish believers questioned him about why he went into the house of Gentiles. Peter carefully explained everything:

  • The vision of the sheet
  • The angel appearing to Cornelius
  • The Holy Spirit coming upon the Gentiles

Then Peter said:

"If God gave them the same gift He gave us when we believed in the Lord Jesus Christ, who was I to oppose God?"

After hearing this, the believers praised God and said:

"God has also granted repentance to the Gentiles."

Important Lessons From the Story

1. The gospel is for everyone
Salvation is not only for Jews — it is for all nations and all people.
2. God sees sincere hearts
Cornelius sincerely sought God through prayer and kindness. God responded to his heart.
3. God changes human traditions
Peter learned that God does not show favoritism. People from every nation can come to God.
4. The Holy Spirit confirms God's acceptance
When the Holy Spirit came on Cornelius' house, it proved that Gentiles were fully accepted by God.

Simple Summary

Cornelius was a Roman officer who feared God and prayed regularly.
God sent an angel telling him to invite Peter.
At the same time, God gave Peter a vision teaching that no person is unclean.
Peter came to Cornelius' house and preached about Jesus.
The Holy Spirit came upon the Gentiles, and they were baptized.
This event showed that salvation through Jesus is for all people, not only Jews.
